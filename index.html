About Us

The college is in Mumias Sub-county of Kakamega County. It is established on a large piece of land of about eleven acres. It is situated at the Mumias Anglican church of Kenya (ACK) Diocesan Headquarters compound. It is next to the magnificent Bishop James Hannington Memorial Cathedral. The college is about 500meters from the Mumias Town Bus stage off Mumias-Musanda road ( Nabongo street) . It is about 25km from Kakamega town. The college was established in the year 2009 after the approval by the Mumias ACK diocesan synod. It is the first of its kind, a teachers’ training college sponsored by the Anglican Church in western Kenya. The region has had a lot of problems in the quest for college training. Students had to travel to very distant places in search of training and at very costly rates. It is on this backdrop that the thought of starting a teacher training college was based. The college was a big solution to the problems of the parents in the county in terms of cost and quality middle level training. There has been a steady increment in the student population due to the high quality training standards offered at the college and evidenced in the good results. Our students are of very high discipline and this has enabled the college to post excellent results in the Primary Teachers Examination [PTE]. Bishop Hannington Teachers’ Training College is a national teacher training college hosting adult people from all over the country and is built on a strong religious base. The “product” realized is found fit for national consumption. The moral and professional ethics for teachers are imparted into the minds of the students to avoid regrets when in employment. All students are expected to behave maturely and co-exist in the college respecting each other’s socio-religious lifestyles. Activities that infringe on other people’s way of living are not condoled. We in essence produce a holistic teacher fit for public consumption anywhere in Kenya and beyond.

BISHOP HANNINGTON TTC MUMIAS GRADUATES AGAIN

The preparation for the second graduation of a Mumias ACK sponsored teachers training college is at a top gear. Bishop Hannington Teachers Training College has lined up a series of activities to mark the much awaited Mumias ACK Diocesan anniversary celebrations due to be held in this year. The occasion to be held at the Diocesan headquarters is highly expected to be presided over by the top most Anglican Church leader the archbishop Eliud Wabukala among other principals. According to one of the institutions directors Mr. Joseph Otsyulah said that the college established in 2009 is first of its kind teachers training college sponsored by the Anglican Church in western Kenya. Briefing the press Mr. Otsyulah said that the college will present about 100 graduates this November compared to 62 graduates of the year 2012. “The increment was as a result of a high quality training standards offered at the college and evidenced in the good results” said Mr. Otsyulah. “Actually our students are of very high discipline and this enabled the college to post excellent results in the 2012 Primary Teachers Examination [PTE] and will maintain the same years to come. We in essence produce a holistic teacher fit for public consumption anywhere in Kenya and beyond” posed Mr. Otsyulah. The activities lined up to make the celebrations include sporting, choir competitions which Mr. Otsyulah said that will be from various parishes that form Mumias ACK Diocese as well as the college choir. The college is at the moment busy recruiting first year students to join this September. The population is expected to keep increasing and when asked how the administration is prepared to meet the challenge of overpopulation, it was revealed that plans are in place to expand the college further so as to cater for all the needy students who wish to train as P1teachers. Applications for new students are still being accepted from those who qualify having scored a mean grade of C plain in KCSE. The applicants can get invitation letters free of charge direct from the college or by calling the college office on cell phone number 0722823652.
